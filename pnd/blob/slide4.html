Chilling Conclusion: The eastward Big Gulp

Finally, the Planning Board, again acting unilaterally, on its own without the recommendation of Burgis Associates, decided to apply the "effective redevelopment" rule to include (and so make subject to seizure) all four remaining lots to the east of Route 1. The reason given was that "more comprehensive redevelopment" was necessary. This was the final, big gulp of land eaten by The Planning Board Blob.

Extending the redevelopment area to the second row of lots, doubling it in size, was proposed by Planning Board Member Allen Schectel, acting as nonvoting Alternate I member of the board at the July 24, 2019 meeting:

Mr. Schectel did not feel that the 180-foot depth of Lots 2,3 and 4 was sufficient enough with setback. He thinks that all ten lots should be included in the redevelopment area.
—Planning Board minutes, July 24, 2019

Of course, this inclusion, too, was not at all necessary to redevelop the area, rather serving only to maximize the profits of the speculator(s) holding the land, and the developer(s) acquiring it.

After all possible lots were cited for redevelopment, The Planning Board Blob, having eaten everything there was to eat, now ceased its land-devouring activities.
