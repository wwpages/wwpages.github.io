NJ Board of Ed. Told of Scholarship Funded by AR-15 Gun Raffle

This web page contains only material that is a matter of public record, and nothing else.

The Board of Education of West Windsor-Plainsboro Regional School District ("WW-P"), based in West Windsor, New Jersey, were informed in late 2021 of a local organization, also based in that town, offering a High School scholarship, funded in part by the sale (raffle) of an AR-15 semi-automatic assault rifle. In the Spring of 2022, WW-P were further informed that the scholarship was promoted by the District to High School students, without disclosing to them that the proceeds of the AR-15 served to fund the scholarship.

Neither the rifle donor nor the raffle winner were from West Windsor, nor anywhere else in Mercer County. The assault rifle was donated by Cheyenne Mountain Outfitters of Bordentown (Burlington County), NJ. The winner of the raffle, who presumably took receipt of the gun, was Erik Bellish of Florence Township (Burlington County), NJ.

On Memorial Day of this year, May 31, 2021, the West Windsor Policemen's Benevolent Association (PBA) raffled off a Smith & Wesson model M&P ("Military & Police") 15 Sport rifle, with advance advertising of the raffle on Facebook in this March 6, 2021 post [1] which read in entirety:

"Going twice....West Windsor PBA #271 is having a fundraiser raffle for your chance to win a Smith & Wesson M&P 15 Sport Rifle. All proceeds benefit the numerous local, state and nation wide charities we regularly donate to, along with our High School Scholarship program. $20 donation for your chance to win! Must have a valid NJ Firearms ID card and pass all required background checks to redeem the prize. Thank you to Cheyenne Mountain Outfitters for sponsoring the raffle. Accepting cash or Venmo (@west-windsor) just put gun raffle as subject."

Among the uses for the funding cited was the "High School Scholarship program." The raffle drawing took place on Memorial Day [2]. The model of rifle raffled off was Smith & Wesson's version of the AR-15 semiautomatic design [3], presumably modified for compliance in New Jersey.

As it happens, this was the same Smith & Wesson M&P 15 model (likely without the modifications) that 17-year-old Kyle Rittenhouse used in Kenosha, WI in August, 2020 [4], nine months before the West Windsor PBA conducted the raffle drawing.
